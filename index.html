In the U.S., 45% of pregnancies each year are unintended (27% wanted later, and 18% unwanted).5 75% of the reasons for not wanting to be pregnant included concern for or responsibility to other individuals; the inability to afford raising a child; and the belief that having a baby would interfere with work, school or the ability to care for dependents. 50% don’t want to be pregnant due to not wanting to raise a child alone or having problems with husband or partner.5 Availability of birth control and education lower rates of abortion, and cause abortions that do happen to happen early, and safely.

Rates of abortion are nearly equal in countries where the practice is outlawed and in countries where it is allowed (29% vs. 27%, respectively).7 Legal abortion has exponentially increased the safety of the practice through quality control and because people have them earlier (25% were 13 weeks or later in 1970, compared with 12% today). When abortion is outlawed, mothers are increasingly jailed and investigated for natural miscarriages, and maternal mortality rate rises as much as 8-11% due to things like refusal of life-saving medication in case it harms the fetus, not stopping ectopic pregnancies, and increased suicide rates especially in the young.6
